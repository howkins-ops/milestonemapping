# ALCHEMIST 2.0 — Concept & Build Bible
### A future-cyberpunk retelling of *The Alchemist*, powered by Jon Howkins' coaching system and real story.

---

## 0. The one-sentence pitch

> The client jacks into a neon-future quest believing they're chasing a goal. They walk a fixed
> 20-chapter myth, do one coaching exercise per chapter, and hear the Mentor's real stories (Jon's
> life). Their own answers quietly write *their* version of the story — and at the climax, the Vault
> opens onto a mirror showing the person they were on Day One versus who they've become. The treasure
> was the Life Purpose they had the whole time.

**The goal is the bait. The transformation is the catch. The story is *theirs*.**

---

## 1. Why this isn't random — and isn't over-complicated

Two ideas do all the work:

- **Fixed myth-spine.** Every client walks the *same* 20 chapters in the *same* order. The story
  never branches. This is what keeps it coherent and buildable.
- **Answer-fill + mirror finale.** The client's typed exercise answers are saved and woven back into
  later narration. The **Day-One Snapshot** (their goal, why, fear — captured Ch 1–2) is held back
  and reflected at **Ch 19 (The Vault)**. That side-by-side *is* their personalized transformation —
  assembled from their own words, with **no AI branching required.**

Two voices interleave the whole way:
- **The Mentor's true story** — Jon's real life, authored once, fixed. (Testimony.)
- **The Seeker's emerging story** — the client's, built live from their answers.

Recurring motif across all 20 chapters: **the Phoenix** — Jon's actual tattoo and identity anchor.
Burn → ash → rise. It's the loading screen, the save icon, the completion seal.

---

## 2. The world (future-cyberpunk reskin)

The Grid: a rain-soaked neon megacity stacked over a dead industrial Undercity. The client is a
**Runner** (the Seeker) chasing a "score" (their real goal) through the city's districts. The
shadows are corrupted subroutines / ghosts wearing human faces. The essences are signal-states the
Runner can boot into. Mentors are fixers and oracles who transmit from the wire.

Style: dark base, neon edges, painterly-not-cartoon, glitch + scanline accents. Uses the existing
brand neon palette and `src/styles/training.css` cinematic animations.

## 5. The personalization engine (how answers become *their* story)

```
Ch 1–2  capture Day-One Snapshot  { why, treasure(goal), biggest_fear }  → frozen, hidden
Ch 3–18 each exercise saves answers to state.outputs[chapterKey]
        later narration reads earlier answers and quotes them back
          e.g. Broke King speaks the client's own money-fear words
          e.g. Ch 17 lists the shadows using the client's Ch 6–16 phrasing
Ch 19   The Vault: render Day-One Snapshot  BESIDE  current answers
          "You came chasing ___.  You were afraid of ___.
           Look who's standing here now."  → THIS is their transformation story
Ch 20   optional: assemble all answers into a shareable "Your Legend" text export
```

No branching. The spine is constant; the **client's truth is the variable.** This is exactly the
`DayOneSnapshot` + `lifePurpose` mechanic already defined in `mapquest-data-model.ts`.

---

## 6. Consent protocol (non-negotiable)

This material includes real trauma. Every Mentor story is tagged:
- **Testimony** — inspiring beats Jon already teaches with (grit, lost-a-million, Mexico epiphany,
  the door-to-door years, the phoenix/ayahuasca rise, "to inspire"). Told ~as-is, in Jon's voice.
- **Transmuted** — the heaviest material (childhood abuse, suicide attempts, addiction depth) becomes
  the *Seeker's fictional weight and backstory texture* — felt in the world, **never surfaced raw**
  in-app. No verbatim trauma text ships without Jon's explicit per-item say-so.

## 7. MVP build path (reuse existing code)

1. **Data** — expand `src/components/map-quest/questChapters.js` from 4 → 20 cyberpunk chapters
   (location, shadow, essence, exercise id, mentor, answer-slots).
2. **State** — extend `src/components/map-quest/useMapQuestState.js`: `outputs[chapterKey]` already
   persists to `localStorage('milestone-quest:mode-v1')`; add a `dayOneSnapshot` field + a helper to
   read any prior chapter's answers (the answer-fill source).
3. **Components** — follow the proven pattern in `ChapterAnchor.jsx` / `ChapterShadow.jsx`
   (cinematic scenes → exercise inputs → `onComplete(outputs)`). Build chapters incrementally; ship
   Ch 1 → Ch 6 first (a full mini-arc that already pays off the Day-One mirror in miniature).
4. **Climax** — a `Vault` component that diffs `dayOneSnapshot` vs current outputs.
5. **Look** — brand neon palette + `src/styles/training.css`. The "Choose Your World" layer from the
   built components is optional cyberpunk flavor, not branching — keep or drop.

**Recommended build order:** proof HTML (Ch 6) → Bible sign-off → Ch 1–5 (the Call) → Ch 6 shadow →
the Vault → fill in the middle shadows.
