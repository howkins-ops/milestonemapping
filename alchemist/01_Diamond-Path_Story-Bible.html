Part 01 · The Shadows

The five masks — and where they really came from

These aren't invented villains. Each survival mechanism traces to a real chapter of your life. That's what makes them teachable — you've lived every one, and returned from it.

Broke King

→ Power
Real root: watching your parents go bankrupt and fight about money every night; stealing food from a grocery store because the fridge was empty. Scarcity wired in early.
The teaching: Money doesn't create power. Power creates the ability to hold money. The boy who had nothing became the man who made a million — then learned the million was never the safety.

Addict Saint

→ Love
Real root: turning to weed, alcohol, and cocaine to numb decades of pain after abandonment; the quick fixes that promised relief and delivered a prison.
The teaching: The part that escapes isn't evil — it's seeking relief. But relief without truth becomes the cage. Love is what the escape was always reaching for.

Silent Prophet

→ Radiance
Real root: over two decades of silence — suffering alone, never telling anyone, bullied for not being able to read, made to feel stupid, learning to hide the voice.
The teaching: Your voice isn't for validation. It's proof you're done hiding from the calling. The kid who was told he wasn't smart enough wrote the book.

Raging Victim

→ Majesty
Real root: abandonment — sister gone for four years, father starting a new family, coming home to an empty house and a goodbye letter. Pain that begged to become identity.
The teaching: Pain may explain the wound. But pain cannot become the throne. You felt every reason to stay the victim — and chose responsibility instead.

Naive Warrior

→ Joy
Real root: the teenager who moved fast and didn't think — the gang, the corner, the deals, courage with no discernment, motion as a way to outrun the pain.
The teaching: Wisdom is not fear. Wisdom is clean movement. The same drive that ran you toward danger becomes the engine that runs you toward purpose.
Why this matters for the game

When the player meets a mask, they're not meeting a cartoon. They're meeting something the coach behind the game survived. The masks carry authority because they're scar tissue, not theory.
