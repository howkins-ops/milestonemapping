Shift Two · The GPS

Alignment — Your values run your life, like it or not

"Show me your values and I'll predict your future. What you value most becomes what you prioritize daily."

Your values are a hierarchy, not a list — and the one at the top quietly controls your time, your money, and every choice you make. The problem is almost nobody actually knows their real hierarchy. They know the one they wish they had. This exercise closes that gap in three moves.

The values gap

Do these in order. Don't skip ahead or look at step 2 while doing step 1 — the whole power is in the surprise.

① If someone shadowed you

If someone followed you all day, every day, with a camera — based only on what they SAW, what would they say you value?

② What you THINK you value

Now write what you believe your top values are — the ones you'd say out loud.

③ Your actual goals

Write your top goals for this season. Income, recruiting, fitness, the launch — whatever they really are.

The realization

Look at column ① against column ③. The values your behavior actually demonstrates are not the values your goals require. You set "recruit 50 / launch the app / lose 20 lbs" — but your shadowed day shows comfort, distraction, scrolling. That's why off-season goals die: old values keep driving while new goals just sit there. Your goals changed. Your values didn't. Fix that and the habits follow on their own.

Build your priority triangle

Now rebuild on purpose. Put the value that must sit at the TOP of the hierarchy this season at the peak — the one that, if you lived from it, would make every goal inevitable. Stack the supporting values below. This triangle is your GPS: the top controls everything beneath it.

How to read your triangle:

The peak is what you prioritize first when there's a conflict — when comfort and discipline both call, this is who wins. It controls your calendar whether you chose it consciously or not.

Each level supports the one above it. If the goal is "recruit 50," the stack might be Growth → Courage → Consistency → Connection → Influence. Lock those in and 50 isn't a dream — it's inevitable.

The test: does this hierarchy actually produce your goal? If your peak value is "comfort," no goal above survival will ever get hit. Drop the values that don't serve the goal. Build a top 5 that fuels execution.

Shift Three · The Climb

Identity — You fall to the level of who you're being

"You don't rise to the level of your goals — you fall to the level of your identity."

This is the hierarchy of who you're being in any moment — your real Shift #3 triangle. Most people live at the bottom, run by survival, and wonder why goals don't stick. The climb is the work: from Survival Mechanism at the base, up through Awareness and the Higher Self, to Essence at the peak. Tap each level.

The identity triangle — climb it

Read bottom to top. You can't skip levels — Awareness is the rung that lets you climb out of Survival. Tap each to open.

Shift Four · Name It To Tame It

Survival → Essence — Catch the mask

"Survival may protect you, but it also imprisons you. Freedom begins when you choose Essence over fear."

Your masks were forged by fear and rejection — but they hardened into a cage. The way out is to catch one in the act. When you label it, brain activity shifts from the amygdala (fear) to the prefrontal cortex (choice), and the charge drops. You stop being the mask and start watching it.
