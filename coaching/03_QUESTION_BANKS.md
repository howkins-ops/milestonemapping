## The Right Questions — Debbie Ford (10) — a Decision Filter
Run a tough choice through all ten; strong candidate for a standalone "Decision Filter" micro-tool.

1. Will this choice propel me toward an inspiring future or keep me stuck in the past?
2. Will this choice bring me long-term fulfillment or short-term gratification?
3. Am I standing in my power or trying to please another?
4. Am I looking for what's right or what's wrong?
5. Will this choice add to my life force or rob me of my energy?
6. Will I use this situation as a catalyst to grow and evolve or to beat myself up?
7. Does this choice empower me or disempower me?
8. Is this an act of self-love or self-sabotage?
9. Is this an act of faith or fear?
10. Am I choosing from my divinity or my humanity?

## Six Coaching Questions — Barry Demp
A tight bi-weekly self-assessment.

1. What is working or going well in your professional and personal life?
2. What is not working, or not where you'd like it to be?
3. What is predictable given the course you're on?
4. What breakthrough results are you committed to producing?
5. What challenges and obstacles are you currently facing?
6. What are the priority areas where you could most benefit from coaching?

## Client Assessment — 10-Dimension Life Scorecard
Rate each 0–10; repeat quarterly; plot the trend. (Source lists 11 items.) High-value for a dashboard scorecard.

1. Effectiveness in life (0 not effective → 10 completely effective)
2. Do your primary environments (home, car, office) support your commitments? (0 → 10)
3. Overall well-being (0 not managed → 10 completely managed)
4. Being in integrity (0 out of integrity → 10 full integrity)
5. Life as a reflection of purpose/core values (0 → 10)
6. Having fun (0 no fun → 10 constant fun)
7. Being at cause in your life (0 → 10)
8. Peace (0 → 10)
9. Love (0 → 10)
10. Overall satisfaction (0 → 10)
11. Inspired about the future (0 uninspired → 10 completely inspired)

*Rating instruction:* account for whether you're a hard/easy grader; be as straight as possible so the
final percentage has value; use results to create/modify projects.

## Career Questions
**Rankings**
1. Rank by importance in a job: title / contribution / money / respect / fame-reputation / integrity
2. More important in a company: profitability / consistency
3. Rank by interest: delivery / development / sales

5. Best at: starting things / maintaining things / completing things
